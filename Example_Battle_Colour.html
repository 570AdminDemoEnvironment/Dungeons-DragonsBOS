⚔️ Example Battle: Party vs. 3 Orcs

A full combat walkthrough using your characters — read this to understand how a fight works!

The Scene: The party is travelling along a forest road toward the Dwarven Excavation. Perseus spots movement in the treeline (his Passive Perception of 14 beats the orcs' Stealth). No surprise round, but the party is aware — everyone rolls initiative!

The Enemies: 3 Orcs — AC 13, HP 15, Greataxe +5 to hit (1d12+3 damage). Special trait: Aggressive — they can use a bonus action to move their full speed toward an enemy (they close distance FAST).

How initiative works: Everyone acts in order from highest to lowest. Each "round" everyone gets one turn. On your turn you can: MOVE (up to your speed) + take one ACTION + sometimes a BONUS ACTION.

Round 1

Orc C Initiative 19

"WAAAGH!" The orc bursts from the trees. It uses its Aggressive trait (bonus action) to sprint its full 30ft toward Halitus, then swings its greataxe.

Attack: d20+5 = 14 vs Halitus's AC 18 → MISS!

The axe clangs off the dragonborn's shield. He doesn't even flinch.

Why it missed: Halitus has AC 18 (chain mail + shield). The orc needed to roll 13 or higher on the d20. It only rolled a 9 (9+5=14). High AC means attacks bounce off!
Perseus Madonicai Initiative 17

Perseus sees the orc standing right next to Halitus. Perfect. He ducks behind a tree and nocks an arrow...

Bonus Action — Cunning Action: Hide
Stealth check: d20+7 = 24 vs Orc's Passive Perception 10 → He's hidden!
Action — Shortbow Attack (with Advantage because hidden!):
Roll twice, take the better: d20+5 = 9, d20+5 = 18 → uses the 18 — HIT!
Damage: Shortbow 1d6+3 = 7 + Sneak Attack 1d6 = 5 → Total: 12 damage!
Orc C: 15 → 3 HP remaining
Sneak Attack explained: Perseus gets bonus 1d6 damage because EITHER he has advantage on the attack OR an ally (Halitus) is within 5ft of the target. He had BOTH here! This only works once per turn — it's the rogue's main damage source.
Halitus Acidspeak Initiative 14

The orc is staggering from the arrow. Halitus raises his warhammer and brings it crashing down.

Action — Warhammer Attack: d20+5 = 17 vs AC 13 → HIT!
Damage: 1d8+5 = 11 (the +5 includes +3 STR and +2 from Dueling fighting style)
☠️ Orc C is DEAD!
Dueling explained: Because Halitus wields a one-handed weapon with a shield, he gets +2 bonus damage on every hit. This makes his average damage very consistent — he doesn't need lucky rolls to hurt things.
Orc A Initiative 12

The second orc sees its companion fall and charges with fury. It uses Aggressive to sprint 60ft and reaches Squelch — the smallest target (it assumes he's the weakest. It's wrong about the armor, but right about the HP).

Attack: d20+5 = 21 vs Squelch's AC 18 → HIT! (rolled a 16 on the die)
Damage: 1d12+3 = 12
💀 Squelch drops to 0 HP — UNCONSCIOUS!
Going to 0 HP: You don't die immediately! You fall unconscious and start making Death Saving Throws on your turns. Your allies can heal you to bring you back. This is dramatic but fixable!
Squelch Puddingpants Initiative 8 — UNCONSCIOUS

Squelch is face-down in the mud. He can't act. He'll start making Death Saving Throws next round.

Orc B Initiative 6

The third orc uses Aggressive to charge toward Perseus in the trees.

Attack: d20+5 = 11 vs Perseus's AC 14 → MISS! (barely!)

The axe chops into a tree trunk as Perseus ducks.

Round 2

Perseus Madonicai Initiative 17

Orc B is right in Perseus's face! Firing a bow this close would be at disadvantage, so he draws his shortsword and lunges.

Action — Shortsword Attack: d20+5 = 16 vs AC 13 → HIT!
Damage: 1d6+3 = 5 (NO Sneak Attack — no ally within 5ft of this orc)
Orc B: 15 → 10 HP
Without Sneak Attack: The rogue's damage drops significantly. This is why positioning matters — Perseus wants to fight the SAME enemy Halitus is fighting, not a separate one.
Halitus Acidspeak Initiative 14

Halitus sees Squelch go down and ROARS. He moves 30ft toward Orc A, opens his jaws, and unleashes his Breath Weapon!

Action — Breath Weapon (Acid, 5×30ft line, 1/short rest):
Orc A makes a DEX saving throw: d20+1 = 8 vs DC 13 → FAILED!
Damage: 2d6 acid = 9 damage (full damage because the save failed)
Orc A: 15 → 6 HP

A stream of hissing green acid erupts from the dragonborn's jaws, searing the orc's chest.

Saving throws vs attack rolls: Some abilities make the TARGET roll to avoid it (saving throw) instead of YOU rolling to hit. Breath Weapon forces a DEX save. If the orc had passed (rolled 13+), it would take HALF damage (4 instead of 9).
Orc A Initiative 12

Wounded and enraged, the orc turns from the downed halfling to face the dragonborn.

Attack: d20+5 = 11 vs Halitus's AC 18 → MISS!

Another swing deflected. Halitus is a wall of steel and scales.

Squelch — Death Saving Throw Initiative 8
Death Save: d20 = 14 → 10 or higher = ✓ Success! (1 of 3 needed to stabilize)
Death Saves: Each turn while at 0 HP, roll a d20. 10+ = success, 9 or less = failure. Three successes = you stabilize (unconscious but alive). Three failures = you die. A Natural 20 = you wake up with 1 HP! A Natural 1 = counts as two failures.

Damage: 1d12+3 = 10

The greataxe connects. Perseus crumples into the undergrowth. Halitus is alone against two wounded orcs, with both allies bleeding out.

Two allies down. Halitus makes a call: kill Orc A fast, then save Squelch — because Squelch can save Perseus.

The warhammer caves in the orc's skull. Two down, one to go. Halitus moves toward Squelch and braces for Orc B's charge.

Strategy: Halitus could have used his Healing Potion on Squelch — but that uses his Action, meaning he can't attack. Instead he kills the orc first (removing a threat) and trusts Squelch to survive one more Death Save. Smart play!
Orc A Initiative 12 — DEAD

Dead. Skipped.

Squelch Puddingpants — Death Save Initiative 8
Death Save: d20 = 20 → 🎉 NATURAL 20!! Squelch wakes up with 1 HP!

Squelch's eyes snap open! He gasps, coughs mud, and sees Perseus bleeding out 35 feet away. He knows exactly what to do.

Bonus Action — Healing Word (range 60ft, costs 1 spell slot):
Healing: 1d4+3 (WIS) +3 (Disciple of Life) = 9 HP restored to Perseus!
Perseus wakes up at 9 HP! 🎉

Still flat on the ground, Squelch gasps out a prayer. Golden light streaks across the battlefield and wraps around Perseus, whose eyes flutter open.

Action — Sacred Flame vs Orc B (60ft, DEX save):
Orc saves: d20+1 = 7 vs DC 13 → FAILED!
Damage: 1d8 radiant = 6
Orc B: 10 → 4 HP
THIS is why the Cleric is incredible. In one turn he: woke up from death (nat 20), healed an ally from 60ft away using a BONUS action (Healing Word), AND dealt damage with his main action (Sacred Flame). Healing Word being a bonus action is game-changing — you can heal AND do something else!
Disciple of Life: Whenever Squelch casts a healing spell, the target recovers an EXTRA 2 + spell level HP. That's why his Healing Word heals 1d4+6 instead of just 1d4+3.

✓ Abilities Showcased in This Fight

  • Sneak Attack — massive damage when an ally is adjacent to target
  • Cunning Action: Hide — bonus action stealth for advantage on next attack
  • High AC tanking — Halitus's AC 18 deflected most attacks
  • Dueling fighting style — consistent +2 damage on every hit
  • Breath Weapon — big area damage, DEX save for half
  • Death Saving Throws — successes, failures, and the dramatic Nat 20
  • Healing Word — bonus action heal from 60ft, brought an ally back from 0 HP
  • Disciple of Life — extra healing on every heal spell
  • Sacred Flame — ranged damage using a saving throw, ignores cover
  • Orc Aggressive — closing distance with bonus action movement

Key Lessons for Your First Session

  • Halitus is the shield — enemies bounce off his AC 18. He should stand in front.
  • Perseus hits like a truck when set up — but dies fast if caught alone. Fight the same enemies as Halitus!
  • Squelch is the glue — one Healing Word can completely reverse a bad situation.
  • Positioning matters — Sneak Attack needs an ally within 5ft of the target.
  • Going to 0 HP isn't death — it's dramatic! Your allies can save you.
  • Action vs Bonus Action — some things (Healing Word, Cunning Action) use your bonus action, so you can STILL do your main action too!
  • Saving throws work backwards — instead of YOU rolling to hit, the ENEMY rolls to dodge. Low roll = they fail = full damage.
