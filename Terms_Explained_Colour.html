Character Sheet — Every Term Explained

Going through the character sheet top to bottom. If you understand these, you can play D&D.

The Header (Top of the Sheet)

Character Name
Your character's name in the game world. This is who you're pretending to be.
Class & Level
Class = your character's job/specialty (Fighter, Rogue, Cleric). It determines what you're good at.
Level = how experienced you are. Everyone starts at Level 1. You level up by completing quests.
A "Fighter 1" is a Level 1 Fighter — fresh adventurer, but already trained for combat.
Race
Your character's species. Each race gives bonuses — Elves are nimble, Dragonborn are powerful, Halflings are lucky.
A Wood Elf gets +2 Dexterity, darkvision (sees in the dark), and moves faster (35 ft instead of 30 ft).
Background
What your character did before becoming an adventurer. Gives you extra skill proficiencies and flavour.
A "Criminal" background means you were a thief — you know how to pick locks and deceive people.
Proficiency Bonus (+2)
A flat bonus added to things you're trained in (proficient). At Level 1, it's always +2. It goes up as you level.
If you're proficient in Stealth and have DEX +3, your Stealth check is: d20 + 3 (DEX) + 2 (proficiency) = d20 + 5.
Hit Dice
The type of dice you roll when healing during a short rest. Also determines your starting HP. Bigger = tougher.
Fighter has 1d10 (tough), Rogue has 1d8 (medium), Wizard would have 1d6 (fragile).

Combat Stats (The Big Numbers)

Armor Class (AC)
How hard you are to hit. When an enemy attacks you, they roll a d20 — if their result is equal to or higher than your AC, they hit. Higher AC = harder to hit.
Your AC is 18. An orc swings at you and rolls a 15 — that's lower than 18, so it misses! If it rolled 19, it would hit.
AC comes from armor + shield + DEX modifier (for light/medium armor).
Hit Points (HP)
Your health bar. When you take damage, subtract it from your HP. When you reach 0, you fall unconscious and start making death saves. You're NOT dead yet at 0 — your allies can heal you.
You have 12 HP. An enemy deals 5 damage. You now have 7 HP. Another hit for 8 damage drops you to 0 — you fall unconscious.
Initiative
Determines turn order in combat. When a fight starts, everyone rolls d20 + their Initiative modifier. Highest goes first.
Fight starts! Rogue rolls d20 + 3 = 17. Fighter rolls d20 + 2 = 9. Goblin rolls 12. Turn order: Rogue → Goblin → Fighter.
Initiative = your DEX modifier. Quick characters act sooner.
Speed
How far you can move on your turn, in feet. A grid square = 5 feet. So 30 ft speed = 6 squares of movement per turn.
Speed 30 ft: you can move 6 squares on a grid. Speed 35 ft (Wood Elf): you can move 7 squares.

Ability Scores (The 6 Stats)

Every character has 6 core stats. Each has a Score (the raw number, like 16) and a Modifier (the bonus you actually add to rolls, like +3). The modifier is what you use in play.
How to get the modifier: Score minus 10, divide by 2, round down. (Don't worry — it's pre-calculated on your sheet!)
Score 16 → (16 - 10) ÷ 2 = +3. Score 8 → (8 - 10) ÷ 2 = -1.
Strength (STR)
Physical power. Used for: melee attacks with heavy weapons, breaking things, climbing, lifting, shoving.
Kicking down a door? Roll d20 + STR modifier.
Dexterity (DEX)
Agility and reflexes. Used for: ranged attacks (bows), finesse weapons, dodging, sneaking, picking locks, initiative.
Sneaking past a guard? Roll d20 + DEX modifier (+ proficiency if trained in Stealth).
Constitution (CON)
Toughness and endurance. Used for: hit points, resisting poison, holding your breath, maintaining concentration on spells when hit.
A higher CON means more HP. CON +3 = you get 3 extra HP at every level.
Intelligence (INT)
Book smarts and memory. Used for: recalling lore, investigating clues, arcane magic (for Wizards).
Trying to figure out what ancient runes mean? Roll d20 + INT modifier.
Wisdom (WIS)
Awareness, intuition, and street smarts. Used for: noticing things (Perception), reading people (Insight), healing magic (for Clerics), survival.
Noticing an ambush? That's Perception = d20 + WIS modifier + proficiency (if trained).
Charisma (CHA)
Force of personality. Used for: persuading, deceiving, intimidating, performing, and some magic (Bards, Sorcerers).
Trying to talk your way past a guard? Roll d20 + CHA modifier (+ proficiency if trained in Persuasion).

Saving Throws

What's a Saving Throw?
A saving throw is a reaction roll — something bad is happening TO you and you're trying to resist it. The DM tells you which ability to roll. You roll d20 + that ability's modifier (+ proficiency if your class is proficient in that save).
A trap shoots fire. DM says: "Make a Dexterity saving throw." You roll d20 + DEX mod. If you beat the DC (difficulty), you dodge and take half damage.
How is this different from Ability Scores?
Ability Score/Modifier = your raw stat. Used for skill checks (things YOU choose to do).
Saving Throw = same modifier, BUT you might also add your proficiency bonus (+2) if your class is proficient in that save. This is class training for reacting to dangers.

Think of it this way:
• Skill check = "I want to do something" (you initiate)
• Saving throw = "Something is happening to me, can I resist?" (you react)
A Rogue has DEX +3. Their DEX saving throw is +5 (because Rogues are proficient in DEX saves: +3 DEX + 2 proficiency). But a Cleric with DEX +1 only gets +1 on DEX saves (not proficient).
The ★ symbol
★ means your class is proficient in that saving throw — you add your proficiency bonus (+2) on top of the ability modifier. Each class gets 2 proficient saves.
Fighter is proficient in STR and CON saves. Rogue in DEX and INT. Cleric in WIS and CHA.

Skills

What's a Skill Check?
When you try to do something with an uncertain outcome, the DM asks for a skill check. You roll d20 + the relevant skill modifier. Beat the DC (number the DM picks based on difficulty) and you succeed.
You try to climb a cliff. DM says: "Make an Athletics check, DC 12." You roll d20 + Athletics modifier. Got a 14? You made it!
How Skill Modifiers Are Calculated
Each skill is tied to an ability score. Your modifier = ability mod + proficiency (if proficient, marked ★).
Stealth is tied to DEX. If you have DEX +3 and are proficient (★): Stealth = +3 + 2 = +5. If NOT proficient: just +3.
Expertise (★★)
Some characters (like Rogues) get Expertise — they add proficiency TWICE instead of once. This is why Perseus has Stealth +7 (DEX +3 + proficiency ×2 = +3 + 4).

Weapon Attacks

To Hit
The bonus you add when rolling to attack. You roll d20 + To Hit. If the total meets or beats the target's AC, you hit them.
Your "To Hit" is +5. Enemy AC is 13. You roll d20 and get an 8. Total: 8 + 5 = 13. That equals the AC — you hit!
To Hit = ability modifier (STR for melee, DEX for ranged/finesse) + proficiency bonus.
Damage
If you hit, roll this to see how much HP you take away from the target. Written as dice + modifier.
Damage is "1d8+5". Roll an 8-sided die (say you get a 6), add 5 = 11 damage. The enemy loses 11 HP.
Damage Type
What kind of damage (piercing, slashing, bludgeoning, fire, etc.). Usually doesn't matter — BUT some creatures resist or are vulnerable to certain types.
Ochre jellies are resistant to slashing — and they SPLIT into two if you hit them with a sword! That's why Halitus uses a warhammer (bludgeoning) instead.

Features & Abilities

What Are Features?
Special things your character can do because of their class or race. Some are always active (passive), some you choose to use (active). Active ones often have limits — "1/short rest" means you can use it once, then it recharges when you take a rest.
Second Wind (Fighter): "As a bonus action, heal 1d10+1 HP. 1/short rest." = You can heal yourself once per rest period.

Spellcasting (Cleric Only)

Spell Save DC
When you cast a spell that forces an enemy to resist, they roll a save against this number. If they roll below your Spell Save DC, your spell fully works.
Squelch casts Sacred Flame (DC 13). The goblin makes a DEX save and rolls 11 — that's below 13, so it takes the full 1d8 radiant damage.
Spell Save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + WIS modifier.
Spell Attack Bonus
Like "To Hit" but for spells that require an attack roll (instead of a saving throw). Roll d20 + Spell Attack vs. enemy AC.
Spell Attack = proficiency bonus + WIS modifier.
Spell Slots
Your "spell fuel." Each non-cantrip spell costs 1 slot to cast. At Level 1, you have 2 slots. Once used, they're gone until you take a long rest (sleep 8 hours). You must choose wisely.
You have 2 slots. Combat starts — you cast Bless (1 slot used, 1 remaining). An ally drops to 0 HP — you cast Healing Word (last slot used, 0 remaining). Now you can only use cantrips until you sleep.
Cantrips
Simple spells you can cast unlimited times — no spell slot needed. They're your "basic attacks" as a caster.
Sacred Flame: unlimited uses, deals 1d8 damage at 60ft range. Use it every turn when you don't need to heal.
Concentration
Some spells say "Concentration" — this means you must actively maintain them. Rules: (1) Only ONE concentration spell at a time. (2) If you take damage while concentrating, roll CON save (DC 10 or half the damage, whichever is higher) — fail and the spell ends.
Squelch is concentrating on Bless. An orc hits for 8 damage. Roll CON save DC 10 (since 10 > 8÷2=4). Beat 10? Bless stays active. Fail? It ends.

Senses

Passive Perception
How observant you are WITHOUT actively trying. The DM uses this to determine if you notice hidden things automatically — without you asking to look.
A hidden tripwire has DC 13 to notice. Your Passive Perception is 14. DM tells you: "You spot a thin wire stretched across the hallway." (You didn't even ask!)
Passive Perception = 10 + Perception modifier.
Passive Insight
How good you are at reading people without actively trying. The DM uses this to tell you if someone seems dishonest.
An NPC lies to the party. Their Deception is 12. Your Passive Insight is 15 — DM tells you: "Something feels off about what they just said."
Darkvision
Ability to see in the dark (in grayscale/black and white). Elves have this naturally. Without it, you need a torch or you're blind in darkness.
The party enters a dark cave. Perseus (Elf) can see fine (60 ft in darkness). Halitus and Squelch need a torch.

Death Saves

How Dying Works
When you hit 0 HP, you fall unconscious. You're NOT dead. Each turn, you roll a d20 (no modifiers):

• Roll 10 or higher = 1 Success ✓
• Roll 9 or lower = 1 Failure ✗
• Roll a natural 20 = you wake up with 1 HP!
• Roll a natural 1 = 2 Failures at once

3 Successes = you stabilize (unconscious but no longer dying)
3 Failures = your character dies
You're at 0 HP. Round 1: roll 14 (1 success). Round 2: roll 6 (1 failure). Round 3: Squelch casts Healing Word for 5 HP — you wake up immediately! Death saves reset.
ANY healing at 0 HP wakes you up instantly. That's why Healing Word is so valuable.

Your Turn in Combat

The Structure of a Turn
On your turn you get three things:

1. Movement — Move up to your Speed (e.g. 30 ft). You can split this before and after your action.
2. Action — Your main thing: Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash (double movement), Dodge, Disengage, Help, Hide, Use an Object.
3. Bonus Action — Only if you have an ability that specifically says "bonus action." Not everyone has one every turn.
Halitus's turn: Move 20 ft toward the orc (movement), swing warhammer (action), use Second Wind to heal (bonus action). Done!
Action vs. Bonus Action
Action = your main thing each turn. Everyone gets one.
Bonus Action = a quick extra thing. You only get one IF you have an ability that uses it. You can't substitute one for the other.
Squelch casts Sacred Flame (action) AND Healing Word (bonus action) on the same turn — because Healing Word specifically says "bonus action."
Advantage & Disadvantage
Advantage = roll 2d20, take the HIGHER result. Situation is in your favour.
Disadvantage = roll 2d20, take the LOWER result. Situation is against you.
They cancel each other out if you have both.
Perseus is hidden and attacks from shadows → Advantage. Roll two d20s: 7 and 15. Take the 15! (Plus, since he has advantage, he gets Sneak Attack damage.)

Other Terms

Short Rest vs. Long Rest
Short Rest = 1 hour break. You can spend Hit Dice to heal. Some abilities recharge (Second Wind, Breath Weapon).
Long Rest = 8 hours (sleep). Regain ALL hit points, ALL spell slots, ALL abilities. Full reset.
DC (Difficulty Class)
The target number you need to meet or beat on a roll. Set by the DM for skill checks, or by the spell/ability for saves.
Easy = DC 10 | Medium = DC 15 | Hard = DC 20
Proficiency (★)
Being trained in something. When proficient, add your Proficiency Bonus (+2 at Level 1) to the roll. Without proficiency, just use the raw ability modifier.
d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20
Dice with that many sides. "2d6" means "roll two 6-sided dice and add them together." The d20 is the main die — used for almost every check, attack, and save.
Natural 20 / Natural 1
The number shown on the d20 BEFORE adding modifiers.
Nat 20 on an attack = automatic hit + double damage dice (critical hit!).
Nat 1 on an attack = automatic miss, no matter how high your bonus is.
Halitus rolls a 20 on the die. Critical hit! Roll damage dice twice: 2d8+5 instead of 1d8+5. Devastating!
